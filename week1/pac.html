Georgia

( Abkhaz: Қырҭтәыла ) is a state in Eurasia , the Caucasus , on the eastern shore of the Black Sea . It is bordered by Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south , and Azerbaijan to the southeast . The transcontinental country is located at the crossroads of Southeastern Europe and Western Asia , although sociopolitically and culturally part of Europe.


The territory of modern Georgia has been permanently inhabited since the Early Stone Age . In classical antiquity, the early Georgian states of Colchis and Iberia flourished , which gave rise to a common Georgian culture and statehood. Christianity prevailed from the early IV century; The country became a united monarchy in 1008.


However, after that, Georgia went through several periods of revival and decline, until in the XV century it disintegrated into several small political units. Imperial Russia conquered Georgian lands in parts in 1801-1878. An independent country restored after the Russian Revolution - the Democratic Republic of Georgia(1918–1921) fell victim to the Bolsheviks and became part of the Soviet Union in 1922 .
